Can you help me solve for the final velocity of an object in free fall from 50 meters? Ignore air resistance.

science

Certainly. To find the final velocity (v) of an object in free fall from a height (h) of 50 meters, we can use the kinematic equation that relates velocity, acceleration, and displacement, independent of time.

v² = u² + 2gh

Where:

  • u = initial velocity (0 m/s, since it's dropped)
  • g = acceleration due to gravity (approx. 9.81 m/s²)
  • h = height (50 m)

Substituting the values:

v² = 0² + 2(9.81)(50)

v² = 981

v ≈ 31.32 m/s

The final velocity right before impact is approximately 31.32 meters per second downward.
